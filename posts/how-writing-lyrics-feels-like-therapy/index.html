How Writing Lyrics Feels Like Therapy

Music heals, and sometimes writing lyrics feels like sitting on a therapist’s couch with only a pen and a blank page.

How Writing Lyrics Feels Like Therapy

Music? Well, if you are a fan of mine, you most well kow that music is the number 1 way I know conveys mesages and feelings perfectly. So I decided to write an article, on a personal point of view, how writing Lyrics, you know coming up with music, feels like therapy. So if you are someone who is thinking of starting to write music, or you are an experienced lyricist and you're wondering why you feel the way you do when writing, then you are in the right place. So let's dig in?

Lyrics Are Confessions in Disguise

Now I think and believe that this is something almost everyone has eexperienced. Doubt it? let me ask you this. After they broke your heart, whoever they are, wasn't there this one time that you felt inspired to write a song about them? You picked up a pen, and started writing. They didn't necesarily have to be lyrics at first, they were just honest words, written on a plain blank sheet. Your feelings written on paper. Well that's how lyrics are written, or at least that's how the emotional true ones are.

For some of us, those unspoken words that we write, the ones that mature into great songs, feel like therapy; only in this case, the couch is a blank page and the questions are asked by your own thoughts. In this way, it's like making a confession to the world about something you're feeling inside, but disguised as lyrics, or in other words, music.

Music Turns Pain into Patterns.

These patterns are the ones that help most people make sense of what they are feeling. They are the ones that help people take control and finally understand how to cope with every feeling they have. Whether it's joy, pain, fear, or even happiness. And I'm not talking only about the listener, I'm talking about the lyricist too; and last time I checked, that's what therapy is for.

So keeping this in mind, why exactly does writing lyrics feel like therapy?

  • It validates what you are feeling. Yhp, onc eeit's on paper, it validates what you are feeling.
  • It gives control. You can bend pain into rhyme, rearrange memories into verses, rewrite endings you didn’t get in life. At this very moment, you control destiny. Okay, that's a bit overdone, but you get the point.
  • It creates connection. Even if no one hears it, you’ve spoken. And if someone does hear it and says, “that’s exactly how I feel,” suddenly you’re not alone.

The Main Point

I'll be totally honest with you. Not every lyric is mean for release. Some lines will never leave that notebook, or that journal. They may be too personal, or just for me to read, and some of them weren't even meant to e lyrics. But that's okay. Simply writing them down is enough. Because at the end of the day, lyric writing is not just about making music, no. It's sometimes about surviving those hidden feelings. It's about making peace with what you can never change. And it's also aout giving a voice to that which you can't silence.

So, the next time you’re overwhelmed, try it. Write a line. Doesn’t matter if it rhymes. Doesn’t matter if it’s “good.” Just let it spill. You might be surprised at how much lighter you feel when that simple lyric finally speaks back to you.
