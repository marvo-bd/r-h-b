A quiet, reflection on loving someone long after the story should’ve ended... raw, honest, and human than anything.

The Fact That I Love You

October 16, 2025 • 3 min read

The Fact That I Love You

the fact that i love you
doesn’t make sense anymore.

it’s not poetic.
it’s not cinematic.
it’s not the kind of love people write about in songs.
it’s just... there.
persistent.
ordinary.
like a heartbeat I never asked for but can’t turn off.

and maybe that’s what makes it worse.
the fact that I still think about you
even after deleting every trace of you
but can’t delete the way your name feels in my mouth.

the fact that I still defend you
in conversations you’ll never hear.
that I still pause at 11:11,
like some part of me is thirteen again,
believing the universe still takes requests.

the fact that I love you
means I still flinch at certain songs,
still double-take when I catch your cologne in the air,
still check my phone for messages I know won’t come.

and I hate that love doesn’t need permission.
that it lingers long after logic says “leave.”
that it’s not about wanting you back
it’s about not knowing where to put all this love now
that you’re gone.

I tried to give it to someone else once.
he was kind.
funny.
safe.
but when he said my name,
it didn’t sound like a home I’d lived in before.

and that’s when I realized
the fact that I love you
doesn’t mean I want you.
it just means I still remember how to.

it means I still carry the echo of something real
in a world that keeps telling me to move on.

so no, I’m not waiting for you.
I’m just learning how to love
without needing it to be returned.
learning that some love stories
don’t end, they just change shape.

and maybe, someday,
I’ll stop calling this love.
maybe I’ll just call it
proof
that I once felt deeply
and didn’t run from it.

but for now…
for now, the fact that I love you
is just another truth I live with.
quietly.
honestly.
and maybe,
that’s enough.

The end.
