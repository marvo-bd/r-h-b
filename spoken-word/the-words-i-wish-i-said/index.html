The Words I Wish I Said

hey.
I don’t even know if this will reach you.
maybe it’s better if it doesn’t.
I just—
I needed to let this out somewhere that isn’t my chest.

there are so many things
I wish I said
when it still mattered.

like,
I wish I told you how your laugh
used to feel like sunlight on my skin.
how I memorized your habits
the way some people memorize prayers.
how I was scared—
not of losing you,
but of deserving you
and still watching it fall apart anyway.

I wish I said
that sometimes I was quiet
not because I didn’t care,
but because I was too busy trying to hold myself together
in the same breath that held your name.

I wish I said
that I wasn’t angry—
I was hurt.
and that my silence wasn’t peace,
it was punishment
for not being enough to make you stay.

I wish I said
that I loved you,
not in that easy, movie kind of way,
but in the way that hurts—
the kind that leaves fingerprints on your soul
long after the body forgets.

and I wish I said
that when you left,
I stopped recognizing my own reflection.
I kept looking for traces of you
in the eyes of strangers,
in the coffee I never finished,
in the songs I still skip
because they sound too much like you.

and god,
I wish I said I forgive you.
not because you deserve it,
but because I do.
because I’m tired of carrying your ghost
like proof that I once mattered to someone.

these—
these are the words I wish I said
when I still believed you’d listen.
but I guess I’m saying them now,
into the empty air,
into a space where you used to be.

and maybe that’s enough.
maybe saying it here
means I can finally
let it go.
